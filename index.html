How an Independent Insurance Agent Works

An independent insurance agent represents multiple insurance carriers rather than a single company. When you contact an independent agent in Greenville, SC, they review your coverage needs and compare policy options from several carriers to find the best combination of coverage, price, and service. The agent works for you, not for the insurance company.

Unlike a captive agent who can only offer products from one company, The Morgano Agency shops your coverage across all of its carriers every time your policy renews. If a carrier raises rates, your independent agent can move your coverage to a more competitive carrier without you having to start the process over with a new agency.

Captive Agent vs. Independent Agent

When you buy insurance in Greenville, you are working with one of two types of agents: a captive agent who represents a single insurance company, or an independent agent who represents multiple carriers. Here is how they compare:

Feature Captive Agent Independent Agent
Carrier Options One company only. Can only quote and bind policies from their employer (State Farm, Allstate, Farmers, etc.). Multiple carriers. Compares coverage and pricing from several insurance companies for each line of business.
Who They Work For The insurance company. The captive agent is an employee or exclusive contractor of the carrier. You, the customer. The independent agent is contracted with multiple carriers and is not beholden to any single company.
Price Shopping Cannot shop your rate. If your carrier raises premiums, the captive agent has no alternative to offer. Shops your rate across carriers at every renewal. If one carrier increases premiums, the agent can move your coverage.
Policy Ownership The carrier owns the book of business. If the captive agent retires or leaves, your policy stays with the company. The agency owns the book of business. Your relationship is with the agency, and your coverage follows you regardless of individual agent changes.
Claims Advocacy Limited. The captive agent works for the company that is paying the claim. There is an inherent conflict of interest. Strong. The independent agent advocates on your behalf with the carrier because the agent's loyalty is to you, not the insurance company.
Specialized Coverage Limited to what their carrier offers. If their company does not write a particular type of coverage, you need to find a separate agent. Access to specialized markets. Independent agents can place hard-to-insure risks, surplus lines, and niche coverage through their carrier appointments.

Benefits of Working With an Independent Agent

Multi-Carrier Access

Your independent agent compares auto, home, and business insurance across multiple carriers every time you need a quote or renewal. Different carriers have different appetites, rating algorithms, and discount structures. What is expensive with one carrier may be competitively priced with another.

Claims Advocacy

When you file a claim, your independent agent acts as your advocate with the insurance company. They help you navigate the claims process, push for fair settlements, and escalate disputes. Your agent has leverage because they can move your business to a different carrier if the claims experience is poor.

Coverage Reviews

Life changes affect your insurance needs. Marriage, a new home, a growing business, teenage drivers, retirement. Your independent agent reviews your entire insurance portfolio as your life evolves and adjusts coverage across all carriers to match your current situation.

Local Knowledge

An independent agent in Greenville understands local risks: hail damage patterns, flood zones along the Reedy River, traffic corridors with high accident rates, and the industries that drive the Upstate economy. That local knowledge translates to better coverage recommendations.

One Phone Number

Your auto, home, umbrella, life, and business insurance may be with different carriers, but you call one phone number for all of them: your independent agent. The Morgano Agency manages your entire insurance portfolio from a single point of contact at (864) 609-5285.

No Cost to You

Independent agents are compensated by the insurance carriers through commissions built into your premium. You do not pay a fee to use an independent agent. The price you pay for a policy through an independent agent is the same as buying it directly from the carrier.

How the Independent Agent Process Works

Consultation

You call The Morgano Agency at (864) 609-5285 or visit the office at 206B Pine Knoll Dr in Greenville. Your agent reviews your current coverage, identifies gaps, and gathers the information needed to quote your policies across multiple carriers.

Market Comparison

Your agent submits your information to multiple carriers and collects quotes with varying coverage options, deductible levels, and premium amounts. Each quote is evaluated for coverage quality, not just price.

Recommendation

Your agent presents the options and explains the differences in coverage, exclusions, deductibles, and claims handling reputation among the carriers. The recommendation is based on your specific risk profile, not on which carrier pays the highest commission.

Binding Coverage

Once you select a carrier and coverage option, your agent binds the policy and issues proof of insurance. For auto policies, your SC insurance ID card is available immediately. For business policies, certificates of insurance (COIs) are issued the same day.

Ongoing Service

Your agent is your point of contact for policy changes, claims, coverage questions, and renewals. At renewal, your agent re-shops your coverage to confirm you still have the best rate and coverage from the available carriers.
